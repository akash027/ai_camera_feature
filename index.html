Core AI Camera Capabilities

Ten capabilities every operation can benefit from.

The ten capabilities below cover what most industrial and commercial operations need — safety, security, quality, and productivity. Each one would be built on top of your existing cameras, tuned to your site.

PPE Compliance Detection

Continuous detection of helmets, gloves, high-vis vests, safety shoes, and eye protection. Per-zone rules so each camera knows what counts as compliant in its area.

Worker Safety & Restricted Zones

Virtual fences around machinery, forklift aisles, and restricted zones. Instant alerts when a worker breaches a boundary or approaches unsafe areas.

Fire & Smoke Detection

Visual fire and smoke recognition across storage areas, production lines, and critical zones — often flagging early signals before conventional sensors reach threshold.

Intrusion & After-Hours Security

Perimeter monitoring outside working hours, unauthorised entry into sensitive areas, and tampering at high-value zones — with direct escalation to security teams.

Idle-Time & Productivity

Measures dwell time at each workstation or zone. Flags unusually idle areas, extended absences near active operations, and pattern-level productivity gaps across shifts.

Material Handling & Heavy Loads

Monitors loading and unloading activity, swing detection during hoisting, pedestrian-in-path warnings at weighing stations, and truck-dock safety during dispatch.

Quality Inspection

Detects defects, misalignments, missing components, print or label errors, and other visual anomalies on production lines — before finished goods leave the floor.

Warehouse & Inventory Movement

Rack occupancy heatmaps, aisle congestion monitoring, stacking compliance, anomaly detection in movement patterns, and dispatch-area turnaround tracking.

Vehicle & Logistics Tracking

ANPR-based truck entry/exit logging, dock dwell-time measurement, empty-vs-loaded classification, and automated gate-pass reconciliation with dispatch records.

Environmental & Hazard Monitoring

Detects dust accumulation, oil or chemical spills, water pooling near electrical equipment, blocked fire exits, and general housekeeping deviations for ESG reporting.

Use-Case Scenarios

How the system would behave on a typical day.

Illustrative moments showing how the proposed capabilities would handle common situations once implemented — not past incidents or live examples.

01

Worker enters restricted zone without required PPE

A worker walks into a restricted production area without a helmet or required protective gear. The supervisor is elsewhere on the floor and doesn't see the breach.

→ Alert with a short video clip would be sent to the floor supervisor in near real-time. Worker guided back to compliance before incident occurs.
02

Smoke detected in storage area

Night shift. Thin smoke starts rising from a wiring fault in a storage zone. The conventional smoke sensor has not yet reached threshold — it takes minutes in low-ventilation areas.

→ Visual smoke model would flag the event early. Plant manager and security alerted immediately. Fire team dispatched before spread.
03

Defect detected on a production line

A subtle quality defect appears on a production line. On a normal day, it might be caught at final QC. On a busy day, several units may reach dispatch before anyone notices.

→ Quality model would detect the irregularity at the station. Product flagged, held, and corrected before it leaves the line.
04

Forklift in pedestrian-only aisle

Shift changeover. A forklift takes a shortcut through a designated pedestrian aisle while workers are walking through. No one sees it at the time.

→ Forklift operator and safety lead notified with video. Incident logged automatically. Retraining item added to the weekly safety review.
